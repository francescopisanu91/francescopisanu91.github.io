Francesco Pisanu

Another mathematician with a PhD in Computer Science

I am a potdoctoral fellow in Computer Science, member of CORE (Center for Operations Research and Econometrics), at the Université Catholique de Louvain in Belgium, where I moved from University of Sorbonne Paris Nord in France in 2020.

I have been teacher at the Institut Galilee during the A.Y. 2023/24, and at the IUT Villetaneuse during the A.Y. 2021/22-2022/23.

PhD thesis: On box-total dual integrality and total equimodularity, defended in 2023.

Research interests

Integer programming, combinatorial optimizzation, computational complexity, and graph theory

I am mostly interested in graph theory and polyhedral combinatorics. Most of my efforts are devoted to the study of strong integrality properties of polyhedra such as integer decomposition property and box-total dual integrality. The vast majority of my results are on complexity and involves several classical structures such as the stable sets, (perfect) matchings, edge coverings, and vertex covering. During my postdoc at CORE I started working on network design problems specialized on hierarchical clustering.
